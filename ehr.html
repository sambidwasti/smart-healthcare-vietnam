Electronic Health Records (EHR)

Electronic Health Records (EHR) are secure digital systems used to collect, store, and manage patients' medical histories. They provide healthcare providers with accurate, up-to-date information at the point of care, enhancing the efficiency, quality, and safety of patient treatment.

By centralizing medical data, EHRs reduce paperwork, minimize errors, and improve coordination among healthcare workers. This system supports better clinical decisions, facilitates follow-up care, and ensures seamless communication across different levels of the healthcare system.
